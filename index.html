THE RATTLER and Other Songs

The long-awaited new album from Run Downhill

RUN DOWNHILL is the brainchild of multi-GRAMMY® Award winning artist T.J. Troy, a Los Angeles-based musician/writer/producer, blending his original music, comic book art and narrative, and multimedia live performance into a single integrated ensemble. The group’s newest album continues the natural evolution of T.J.’s writing and production style: 10 new songs, 5 instrumental and 5 lyric-driven, brought to life with dramatic performances from the band’s core trio of T.J. (guitar, baritone guitar, drums/percussion, and voice), Nate LaPointe (electric guitar and pedal steel), and Adam Levy (bass), alongside the multi-talented guest artists joining the trio throughout the collection.

Opening the album with a lush cover rendition of Will Oldham’s “Drinking Woman”, the record mostly alternates between vocal-driven and instrumental songs, often showcasing the talents of the album’s special guests: the intricately delicate “Late for work; running home” alternates melodic salvos between T.J.’s baritone guitar lines and guest Yasmine Azaiez’s brilliant violin, punctuated with LaPointe’s pedal steel and Levy’s understated double bass. “Hungry for Something” features the trio’s rustic American rock n’ roll roots accentuated by organist Phil Parlapiano, followed by the cinematic instrumental title track, “The Rattler,” where layered orchestral strings from Azaiez and cellist Derek Stein accompany T.J.’s baritone guitar to new heights. “This is us, in a play” became the album’s second single, its lyrical story of a couple’s failed courtship unfolding as though on a stage creating a nostalgic mood.

Leon Rothenberg’s gentle processing brings an ethereal quality to “Gone are the days when it rained”, and Clarence “Slide” Spears’ lonesome trombone on “That I were Comatose” sings us home on that long, dark road. Memphis-based guitarist Jeff Jensen brings his blues-driven style to “Spaces that have been filled”, and David Longoria’s sizzling trumpet shines in his own featured melodic instrumental, “Let It Roll”, joined by Joe Garza’s solid rhythm guitar. Natalie Nicole Gilbert sings a final duet with T.J., accompanied by much needed rainfall to bring the album to a close.

Like a flowering wooly rose, this music is brooding, grinding, lonesome, uplifting and beautiful...accompanying the listener on a vision quest through lost deserts of the mind. Like all music from Run Downhill, this music is the perfect accompaniment to long road trips throughout quintessential Americana.
