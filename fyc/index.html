For Your Consideration

BEST AMERICANA ALBUM

THE RATTLER and Other Songs

RUN DOWNHILL

RUN DOWNHILL is the brainchild of multi-Grammy® Award-winning artist T.J. Troy, a Los Angeles-based musician/writer/producer, blending his original music, comic book art and narrative, and multimedia live performance into a single integrated ensemble. The group’s newest album features 10 new songs, 5 instrumental and 5 lyric-driven, brought to life with dramatic performances from the band’s core trio of T.J. (guitar, baritone guitar, drums/percussion, and voice), Nate LaPointe (electric guitar and pedal steel), and Adam Levy (bass), alongside the multi-talented guest artists joining the trio throughout the collection.

In 9 original songs and one cover rendition of Will Oldham’s “Drinking Woman”, the record alternates between vocal-driven and instrumental songs, showcasing writer T.J. Troy’s melodic and lyric sensibilities alongside the talents of the album’s special guests:

  • Yasmine Azaiez - Violin (Late for work; running home, The Rattler)
  • Joe Garza - Rhythm Guitar (Spaces that have been filled, Let It Roll)
  • Jeff Jensen - Lead Electric Guitar (Spaces that have been filled)
  • David Longoria - Trumpet (That I were Comatose, Let It Roll)
  • Phil Parlapiano - Hammond B3 Organ (Drinking Woman, Hungry for Something, Spaces that have been filled)
  • Leon Rothenberg - Effects Processing (Gone are the days when it rained)
  • Clarence “Slide” Spears - Trombone (That I were Comatose)
  • Derek Stein - Cello (The Rattler)

Run Downhill, the perfect accompaniment to long road trips throughout quintessential Americana.
